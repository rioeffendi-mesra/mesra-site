01 — Who we are

Keeping Malaysian industry clean, compliant & connected

Mesra Alam Sekitar Sdn. Bhd. is a DOE iRemote-registered CEMS consultant based in Petaling Jaya, Selangor. Established in 2010, we provide complete Continuous Emissions Monitoring System services to Malaysia's palm oil, energy, cement and manufacturing industries — built around our own data acquisition platform, which integrates with the Department of Environment's iRemote / CEMS 3.0 system so that compliant emission data reaches the regulator continuously.

“

We also helped write the rules we work to.

Mesra served as a Technical Committee Member and Reviewer for the official DOE CEMS Guidelines, and is an appointed member of JSM Working Group NSC 26 / TC 8 / WG 4 — the national body behind Malaysian Standards MS 1596 and MS 2564 — alongside DOE, MPOB, TNB, Petronas and leading Malaysian universities.

03 — What we do

Complete CEMS solutions, from installation to compliance

End-to-end Continuous Emissions Monitoring System services for industrial facilities across Malaysia — helping you stay compliant with the DOE Clean Air Regulations 2014 and avoid costly shutdowns.

CEMS design, supply & installation

Custom-engineered systems for palm oil mills, power plants, cement plants and manufacturing facilities — from site assessment to commissioning.

Calibration, maintenance & spare parts

Scheduled and on-demand servicing that keeps analysers within DOE-accepted tolerances. Multi-brand support — no vendor lock-in — with genuine and compatible parts supplied promptly.

QAL 2 & QAL 3 certification

Formal quality-assurance level testing performed to EN 14181, as required by the DOE for certified CEMS installations.

DOE compliance support

From iRemote connectivity to regulatory inspections, we help you navigate both the paperwork and the people.

Remote monitoring

Our platform transmits live emission data to the DOE's iRemote / CEMS 3.0 system, and gives you real-time CEMS visibility beyond the DOE portal, anytime.

Training & consultation

Upskill your EHS team or get expert advisory on CEMS requirements — from the team that helped write the DOE CEMS Guidelines.
